Phishing Email Analysis Report

1. Introduction

Phishing is a type of cyber attack in which attackers impersonate
legitimate organizations to steal sensitive information such as
usernames, passwords, banking details, and OTPs. These attacks are
usually carried out through emails that appear genuine but contain
malicious links or attachments.

The purpose of this project is to analyze a suspicious email and
identify indicators that reveal whether it is a phishing attempt.

2. Objective

-   Analyze a suspicious email.
-   Examine sender information.
-   Inspect email headers.
-   Detect suspicious links.
-   Identify social engineering techniques.
-   Find technical indicators of phishing.
-   Prepare a detailed phishing analysis report.

6. Phishing Indicators Found

1.  Fake Sender Address Actual Sender: support@paypaI-security.com
    Observation: The domain is paypaI-security.com. Notice the letter I
    instead of l. This is called a homograph attack. Risk Level: High

2.  Generic Greeting Email says “Dear Customer” instead of “Dear Khushi
    Chauhan”. Risk Level: Medium

3.  Sense of Urgency Words like Urgent, Immediately, Suspended, 24
    Hours, Permanent Disable create panic. Risk Level: High

4.  Suspicious URL Displayed Link: Verify Now Actual URL:
    http://paypal-security-login.verify-user.info Problems:

-   Uses HTTP instead of HTTPS
-   Fake domain
-   Contains extra words
-   Not an official PayPal domain Risk Level: High

5.  Reply-To Address Mismatch From: support@paypaI-security.com
    Reply-To: supportverify@gmail.com Legitimate companies rarely use
    Gmail for official communication. Risk Level: High

6.  SPF Failure SPF: Fail Meaning: The sender is not authorized to send
    emails from that domain. Risk Level: High

7.  Missing DKIM DKIM: None Meaning: The email lacks a digital
    signature. Risk Level: High

8.  DMARC Failure DMARC: Fail Meaning: The email failed authentication
    checks. Risk Level: High

9.  Unknown IP Address Originating IP: 185.222.xxx.xxx The IP belongs to
    an unknown hosting provider rather than the legitimate company’s
    mail server. Risk Level: Medium

10. Threatening Language Examples:

-   Account suspended
-   Verify immediately
-   Permanent deletion Attackers use fear to manipulate victims. Risk
    Level: Medium

11. No Personal Information No Customer ID, Name, or Account Number.
    Legitimate organizations usually personalize emails. Risk Level:
    Medium

12. Suspicious Domain Name Official Domain: paypal.com Phishing Domain:
    paypal-security-login.verify-user.info Attackers often create
    lookalike domains. Risk Level: High

7. Social Engineering Techniques Used

-   Fear
-   Urgency
-   Authority
-   Fake Security Alert
-   Account Suspension Threat
-   Emotional Manipulation

8. Technical Analysis Summary

SPF: Failed DKIM: Missing DMARC: Failed HTTPS: Not Used Domain
Reputation: Suspicious Reply-To Match: No URL Safe: No

9. Risk Assessment

Fake Domain – High Suspicious URL – High SPF Failure – High DKIM Missing
– High DMARC Failure – High Urgency – High Fake Sender – High Generic
Greeting – Medium Unknown IP – Medium

Overall Risk: Critical

10. Recommendations

-   Never click suspicious links.
-   Verify sender email addresses carefully.
-   Hover over links before clicking.
-   Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA).
-   Keep antivirus software updated.
-   Use spam filters.
-   Check SPF, DKIM, and DMARC authentication.
-   Report phishing emails to the organization’s security team.
-   Delete suspicious emails immediately.
-   Avoid downloading unexpected attachments.

11. Conclusion

The analyzed email exhibits multiple strong indicators of phishing,
including a spoofed sender address, mismatched reply-to address, failed
SPF and DMARC checks, missing DKIM, suspicious URLs, generic greetings,
and urgent language designed to manipulate the recipient. Based on these
findings, the email should be classified as a phishing attempt with a
Critical risk level. Users should avoid interacting with such messages
and report them through appropriate security channels.
